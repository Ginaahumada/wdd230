Visual Hierarchy

Apple Inc.

Apple.com
On the Apple Inc. website, the principle of visual hierarchy design is prominently evident through the prominent placement and strategic use of visual elements. From the moment a visitor arrives at the site, Apple's iconic logo and navigation bar immediately capture their attention, establishing the brand identity and providing key navigation options. As they scroll down on the homepage, they encounter carefully selected product images and videos, occupying a central and appealing position. These dominant visual elements direct the viewer's gaze towards Apple's featured products. Headlines and text related to these products are presented in a legible and attention-grabbing manner, further complementing the visual hierarchy by providing relevant and engaging information. Collectively, these design elements create a user experience that effectively guides the visitor's attention to the most important aspects of the page, encouraging intuitive exploration and interaction with Apple's products and services.

White Space and Clean Design

Airbnb

Airbnb.com
On the Airbnb website, the application of the White Space and Clean Design principle is clearly evident. The user interface is presented in a minimalist and organized manner, with ample use of white space that creates a sense of clarity and order. This allows important content, such as property images and booking details, to stand out effectively without unnecessary distractions. The typography is legible and simple, making reading and navigation easy for users. Additionally, the layout of elements, such as search filters and booking buttons, is presented intuitively and cleanly, enhancing the user experience when searching for and booking accommodations. Overall, the use of white space and a clean design on the Airbnb page creates a sense of freshness and user-friendliness, contributing to a pleasant and effective user experience.
